RAG rating examples

To help you choose a rating, we've provided some examples of what you might include when RAG (red, amber, green) rating your projects.

The examples do not cover every scenario and are simply to help you decide how to rate a project.

Spend risk

Green

  • No rising costs
  • no slippage
  • Less than 30% expenditure profiled in final year
  • No use of financial flexibility

Amber/Green

  • Project progressing but with minor issues
  • Issues are within control and there is a mitigation plan in place
  • The appropriate RIBA stage has minor slippage but there is mitigation plan in place
  • There is no additional support required

Amber

  • Cost increases of 10% to 20% which are manageable
  • A funding gap exists - a source has been identified but not secured
  • Less than 30% expenditure profiled in final year
  • There is slippage of 20% to 30% between financial years
  • 40% to 50% of expenditure is profiled in the final year
  • The use of flexibility is required but below the threshold

Amber/Red

  • The project has stalled for any reason
  • Value engineering is required or in progress due to significant increases or over-runs
  • Significant additional match funding is required for delivery
  • Potential match funding source has been identified but not secured

Red

  • Major cost increases over 30% which is unmanageable and there is no plan to resolve the problem
  • Major slippage over 40% between financial years
  • Over 60% of expenditure is profiled in the final year
  • Major movement of funding planned
  • Major additional match funding is required to facilitate delivery
  • Match funding source not identified
  • Call in required

Delivery risk

Green

  • Planning is in place
  • Main contract procurement process is complete
  • Delivery milestones on target
  • Appropriate RIBA stage on track
  • Project completed

Amber/Green

  • Project progressing but with minor issues
  • Issues are within control and there is a mitigation plan in place
  • The appropriate RIBA stage has minor slippage but there is mitigation plan in place
  • There is no additional support required

Amber

  • The project is progressing but delayed
  • The issues identified are within control but there is no plan in place to deal with the issue
  • Value engineering is required or in progress
  • Appropriate RIBA stage has slipped considerably
  • Additional support is required

Amber/Red

  • The project has stalled
  • Value engineering is required or in progress and likely to result in a new planning application and delayed delivery timelines
  • Major RIBA stage slippage
  • Major additional support required

Red

  • Project stalled
  • Issues out of control
  • Major support required
  • Major RIBA stage slippage
  • No plan in place
